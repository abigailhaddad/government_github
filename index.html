On September 9, 2024, the Office of Personnel Management issued guidance requiring federal agencies to use plain language job titles in announcements—titles that are "descriptive, organizational, or functional" rather than "generic or jargon-laden." The memo explicitly cited "IT Specialist" as the example of what NOT to do, requiring agencies to use titles like "Cloud Engineer," "Data Scientist," or "Cybersecurity Analyst" instead.

This counter tracks compliance with the Merit Hiring Plan by monitoring days since the last "IT Specialist" posting on USAJobs. We use "IT Specialist" as the benchmark because it was explicitly called out by OPM and is easy to identify automatically.

Note: Just because a job title isn't "IT Specialist" doesn't mean it meets the plain language standard. Other bad GS-2210 titles we've observed include "IT Program Manager," "Computer Systems Administrator (CSA)," and "IT System Administrator" — but we're setting the bar here because it's unambiguous and measurable.
